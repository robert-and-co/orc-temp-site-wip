About ORC

The Ottawa Robotics Competition (ORC) is an annual robotics challenge organized by IEEE Ottawa, bringing together talented students and engineers to design, build, and compete with autonomous robots.

Our competition challenges participants to push the boundaries of robotics engineering, encouraging innovation, teamwork, and technical excellence. Whether you're a seasoned competitor or new to robotics, ORC provides an exciting platform to showcase your skills and learn from peers.

Join us for a day of cutting-edge robotics, networking with industry professionals, and celebrating the spirit of engineering innovation in Canada's capital.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate in ORC?

ORC is open to students, engineers, and robotics enthusiasts of all skill levels. Teams typically consist of 2-5 members, though individual entries may be accepted depending on the competition format.

What do I need to bring?

Teams should bring their own robots, tools, and spare parts. We provide the competition arena, power supplies, and basic facilities. Detailed technical specifications will be released with registration materials.

Are there robot design requirements?

Yes, robots must comply with size, weight, and safety regulations outlined in our competition rulebook. Specific challenge requirements will be announced closer to the event date.

How can I stay updated?

Follow IEEE Ottawa on social media and check back on this website regularly for updates on registration, rules, and event details. You can also subscribe to our mailing list for direct notifications.
